# Outages

There are three kinds of intentional outages:

- Scheduled outage
- One-off non-cycle outage
- Immediate or emergency outage

## Procedures

The procedures from an outage will be mandated by what can run for how long without SAP. The procedure to take down SAP lies within the maintenance purview of the IS team / @JesseMiddleton.

In short, an SAP outage won't explicitly stop any activity on the floor, but it will make activities that are needed not possible to occur.

### Requirements of business

To be determined by the business. For example, DLP can have an outage while dryers are running. This is possible because over that time, there is no interactivity between SAP or associated systems while drying; just at the start and finish.

Each group needs to understand what these requirements for them are, but they should be very similar to the outage window that occurred under M3.

### Requirements of IT

IT will need to:
- Communicate known down time in advance
- Actually turn off the system as expected
