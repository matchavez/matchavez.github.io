# Release Management through Tumu Go-Live

> Staged release management tasks will be moved onto Synlait in a graduated manner to ensure Accenture efficiency and control, while also ensuring communication to Synlait. As the transition occurs, everyone should benefit through a cleaner build process and better outcome.

## Current Status

Accenture have clearance to release transports into SAP, though as a courtesy we are ensuring that any release is coordinated to not take down QA.

## Approval of Transports required (After "Data Load 6")

Once we reach the deployment of what we believe is all issues handled or deferred, specific rules come into place:

- All Transports must have an associated Jira ticket
- The Jira ticket must identify the purpose for each transport
- Any transport must declare and schedule any downtime caused or proposed
- All transports must have the approval of the Release Manager, or Test Manager in absentia.

The approval point will be `SQ1-100` as the point of entry until the final Dress Rehearsal. This will be discussed with Accenture to potentially move back to SD1 depending on the decommissioning process of ST1.

## Post Go-Live

The same deployment rules stay in place until Hypercare is declared over. Afterwards, all Transports must go through the IS CAB procedure / BAU.
